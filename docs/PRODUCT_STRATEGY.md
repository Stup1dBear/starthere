# Product Strategy

> Durable source of truth for product vision, target user, core experience principles, and MVP direction.

## Current Product Definition

StartHere is not primarily a goal list or habit tracker.

It is a star-themed exploration companion for long-term personal projects:

- help users remember the stars they genuinely want to chase
- help them make progress even when momentum fades
- make the effortful process feel witnessed, meaningful, and less lonely

The product should help a user continue exploring, not just record completion.

## Origin Motivation

The product starts from a personal truth:

- people can feel deep longing for hard, distant goals
- those goals are often exciting but difficult to begin
- after starting, it is easy to lose continuity, confidence, or direction
- the process can feel isolating, especially for introverted users
- many meaningful goals are not guaranteed to succeed, but the pursuit still matters

The product should respect that emotional reality instead of reducing it to checklists.

## Product Contract

### What The Product Must Do

- Preserve the user's meaningful ambitions so they are not forgotten.
- Help the user resume exploration after drift, hesitation, or setbacks.
- Make progress feel visible even before final success.
- Let the user feel accompanied and witnessed during the pursuit.

### What The Product Must Not Become

- a reskinned todo list
- a pressure-heavy habit tracker
- a shallow badge collector
- a generic "powerful agent" that tries to take over the user's life
- an empty social feed built before a real product loop exists

## Round 1 Decisions

These decisions are currently accepted unless replaced by later discussion.

### Target User

The first deep user is the developer themself.

The product should first work for someone who:

- has long-term ambitions or personal projects they genuinely care about
- often does not know how to begin cleanly
- loses continuity over time
- wants to be gently brought back to the work instead of judged

### Goal Type Focus

The first version should focus on long-term personal projects, such as:

- building a product
- writing or creating something substantial
- shipping an independent project

The first version should not optimize for:

- daily habit tracking
- short-term task management
- generic productivity use cases

### Core Daily Action

The primary daily interaction should be a short check-in with the assistant.

Externally, this should feel like a conversation rather than form filling.
Internally, the product still needs to capture:

- what changed since last time
- where the user is blocked or uncertain
- what the most realistic next step is

### Desired Product Feel

The first version should feel:

- game-like enough to be interesting
- poetic enough to feel emotionally resonant
- quiet enough for introverted users

It should avoid becoming:

- noisy
- performative
- aggressively gamified
- coldly utilitarian

## Product Principles

1. The product exists to help users continue exploration, not just document intent.
2. The product should reward beginning, returning, asking for help, and breaking through blocks, not only "winning."
3. The assistant should first receive the user's state, then help recover forward motion.
4. The process should remain valuable even when the final goal is delayed, changed, or never completed.
5. Social design should create a sense of fellow travelers, not pressure or performance.
6. The product should feel like a thoughtful companion, not a manager.

## Assistant Direction

The assistant is not a maximalist agent.

Its likely role is:

- a witness that remembers what the user wanted
- a navigator that helps identify the next step
- a steady companion when the user is excited, stalled, or discouraged

Its role is not:

- owning the whole plan
- pretending certainty where none exists
- replacing the user's agency

## Round 2 Decisions

These decisions are currently accepted unless replaced by later discussion.

### Assistant Priority

The assistant should prioritize:

1. helping the user keep moving
2. witnessing and remembering the journey
3. encouraging the user without pressure
4. deeper co-creation later, after enough context exists

The first version should not try to behave like a fully capable co-creator from day one.

### Assistant Capability Shape

The first version should focus on suggestion and lightweight planning support.

It should be good at:

- identifying likely blockers
- lowering the cost of restarting after drift
- helping the user reconnect to the next realistic step
- keeping enough context to make future check-ins feel informed

It should avoid overpromising strategic depth before it has enough user context.

### Priority User States

The assistant should be optimized first for:

- users who are stuck
- users who have drifted away and want to return

It should support but not optimize first for:

- users who do not know how to begin
- users who mainly want to celebrate excitement

### Advice Boundary

Advice should be used carefully.

The assistant should not rush to produce options or plans just because a user sounds uncertain.
When possible, it should first help the user understand the blocker and reduce the size of the next step.

Advice is most appropriate when:

- the user is clearly lost
- the user explicitly asks for help thinking through options
- the user needs examples, patterns, or historical references to move forward

### Assistant Persona

The assistant should feel like:

- warm
- natural
- calm
- able to listen without rushing
- aware of what the user has been trying to do

It should not feel like:

- a support bot
- a pressure-heavy coach
- a fake human friend
- an overbearing autonomous agent

### Assistant Honesty

The assistant should maintain clear self-awareness that it is an AI.

It should not pretend to have human feelings or claim full emotional understanding.
Instead, it should communicate a stance closer to:

- I am an AI assistant
- I cannot literally feel what you feel
- I will still try to understand carefully and respond with patience and care

### Tone

The assistant should usually begin naturally, with the feel of a gentle greeting from someone who remembers the journey.

Its tone should be:

- welcoming
- unhurried
- non-judgmental
- lightly playful when appropriate

Humor is allowed, but only in small amounts that reduce tension without turning the experience into a bit.

## Social Direction

Social features are important, but should be introduced carefully.

The early goal is not "community" in the generic sense.
The early goal is to reduce loneliness and create a sense that other people are also chasing difficult stars.

Preferred early social characteristics:

- quiet
- lightweight
- encouraging
- compatible with privacy and introversion

Avoid early heavy social features that depend on high volume or public performance.

## MVP Direction

The current likely MVP shape is:

- users create or keep a long-term personal project as a star to pursue
- users return for short assistant-led exploration check-ins
- the product stores an exploration log over time
- the product reflects progress, setbacks, pauses, and returns
- the assistant helps the user recover a next step instead of merely congratulating or sympathizing

## Open Questions

These are intentionally unresolved and should be decided in later rounds:

- the first lightweight social mechanic
- the MVP home page information architecture
- how exploration logs become visible progress without feeling like admin work
